MARCEL BREUER

Marcel Lajos Breuer was an American architect, furniture designer and interior architect of Hungarian descent. In 1920 Breuer started at the Bauhaus as a student in the carpenter's department. In 1921 he designed the African chair and in the following year, 1922, the wooden slatted chair. This last seat will be considered as one of the most important products of the Bauhaus. He was in 1923 involved in the design of the experimental house Haus am Horn that developed was for that year's Bauhaus exhibition.

Breuer's most famous chair, de Wassily is inspired by the handlebars of a bicycle..
